# v3.7 cascade — honest 5-fold TLS-FP analysis (2026-05-13)

The previously documented "31.7 % TLS-FP" rate in `FP_REDUCTION_RESULTS.md`
was fold-0-only. With the per-slide eval outputs from the 5-fold CV
(2026-05-13), we can compute the honest 5-fold FP rate.

## Per-fold TLS-FP rates (thr=0.5, min_size=2, closing=1)

| Fold | n GT-neg | n FP | TLS-FP rate |
|------|----------|------|-------------|
| 0    | 41       | 13   | **31.7 %**  |
| 1    | 40       | 16   | **40.0 %**  |
| 2    | 42       | 13   | 31.0 %      |
| 3    | 39       | 12   | 30.8 %      |
| 4    | 44       | 26   | **59.1 %**  |
| **Total / mean** | **206** | **80** | **38.8 %** |

The 5-fold mean is **38.8 %**, ~7 pp higher than the fold-0-only claim.
Fold 4 is a 59 % outlier (more than 1.5× the typical rate).

## Per-cancer-type 5-fold TLS-FP

| Cohort | n GT-neg | n FP | FP rate |
|--------|----------|------|---------|
| BLCA   | 68       | 36   | **52.9 %** |
| KIRC   | 109      | 25   | 22.9 %  |
| LUSC   | 29       | 19   | **65.5 %** |

- **LUSC** has the highest FP rate (65.5 %), consistent with its lowest
  TLS Dice (0.529 ± 0.024 in the 5-fold CV). The cascade systematically
  over-fires on LUSC negatives.
- **BLCA** is also poor (52.9 %).
- **KIRC** is significantly better (22.9 %), aligned with its strong
  GC performance (0.904 ± 0.037).

LUSC has only 29 GT-negative slides across 5 folds, so the rate is
noisier than for BLCA/KIRC.

## Confidence distribution of FPs

Across all 80 FP slides:

- Median patches fired per slide: **2**
- Min: 1, Max: 55
- ~50 % of FPs fire on 1–2 patches (likely incidental noise; hard to
  suppress without losing recall)
- A few outliers fire heavily (15–55 patches) — these are systematic
  FPs that no post-proc can fix

## Recommendations

1. **Update production claim**: cite the honest 5-fold TLS-FP rate of
   **38.8 % (± fold variance up to 59 %)**, not the fold-0-only 31.7 %.
2. **Pathologist review** of the 14 high-confidence FP slides (≥ 5
   patches fired) is the next concrete step for FP-rate improvement.
3. **Cohort-specific deployment**: KIRC is the strongest cohort
   (FP-rate 23 %, GC Dice 0.90); LUSC is the weakest (FP-rate 66 %,
   TLS Dice 0.53). Production may want to flag LUSC predictions
   for extra review.
4. **Fold 4 anomaly**: the 59 % FP rate is anomalous. Worth checking
   whether fold-4-specific cohort or split causes this — could be a
   class-balance edge case in the patient assignment.
